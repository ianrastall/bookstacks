title: "SILENT SCENE"
chapter_order: 10

The Governor stands in the center rigid as a post, with outstretched hands and head
thrown backward. On his right are his wife and daughter straining toward him. Back of
them the Postmaster, turned toward the audience, metamorphosed into a question mark.
Next to him, at the edge of the group, three lady guests leaning on each other, with a
most satirical expression on their faces directed straight at the Governor's family. To
the left of the Governor is Zemlianika, his head to one side as if listening. Behind him
is the Judge with outspread hands almost crouching on the ground and pursing his lips as
if to whistle or say: "A nice pickle we're in!" Next to him is Korobkin, turned toward
the audience, with eyes screwed up and making a venomous gesture at the Governor. Next
to him, at the edge of the group, are Dobchinsky and Bobchinsky, gesticulating at each
other, open-mouthed and wide-eyed. The other guests remain standing stiff. The whole
group retain the same position of rigidity for almost a minute and a half. The curtain
falls.
